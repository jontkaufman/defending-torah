# Design: "Jesus Gave Us New Commandments That Replaced the Torah" Objection Post

**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Type:** Objection response
**File:** `content/objections/jesus-new-commandments.md`

## Purpose

Address the widespread claim that Jesus gave new commandments that replaced Torah. Show through rapid-fire examples that Jesus' "new" teachings are actually Torah explanations and deepenings, not replacements or innovations.

## Core Thesis

Yeshua was a Torah teacher, not a Torah replacer. Every teaching that sounds like a new commandment either directly quotes Torah or explicates what Torah always included. When Yeshua said "but I say," he was explaining Torah's full meaning, not overriding it.

## Approach

**Structure:** Smoking gun opening + rapid-fire examples + brief conclusion

**Tone:** Firm, non-antagonistic, intellectually confident. Steelman why each teaching sounds new before showing Torah roots.

**Scope:** 10 carefully selected examples
- 3 slam-dunks (direct quotes) to establish pattern
- 7 Torah deepenings that address real objections (Sermon on Mount, internal righteousness, etc.)

**Format:** Each example 50-100 words. "One punch hit and run."

**Flow:** Chronological through Jesus' teaching ministry in the Gospels

### 2. Opening (Smoking Gun)

**Goal:** Establish the pattern with the clearest possible example

**Content (2-3 sentences):**
- When asked "which commandment is greatest," Jesus answers by quoting Deuteronomy 6:5 and Leviticus 19:18
- These are often taught as Jesus' new summary, but they're verbatim Torah quotes
- This reveals the pattern: Jesus' "new" teachings are Torah explanations

**Transition:** "This isn't an exception. Here are 10 examples showing Jesus taught Torah, not a replacement for it."

### 3. Ten Rapid-Fire Examples

**Format for each:**


**The 10 Examples:**

#### Slam-Dunks (Establish Pattern)

1. **Greatest Commandment**
   - Yeshua: Matt 22:37-39
   - Sounds new: Presented as Jesus' innovative summary
   - Torah: Deut 6:5 + Lev 19:18 (verbatim quotes)
   - Connection: Not a summary replacing Torah—two Torah commands Yeshua identifies as foundational

2. **Golden Rule**
   - Yeshua: Matt 7:12 "Do to others what you would have them do to you"
   - Sounds new: Seems like Jesus' unique ethical principle
   - Torah: Lev 19:18 "love your neighbor as yourself"
   - Connection: Hillel (1st century BCE) gave negative form ("what is hateful to you, do not do to another"). Yeshua gives positive form. Both are explicating Lev 19:18. This was active Torah teaching tradition.

3. **Honor Parents**
   - Yeshua: Mark 7:9-13, defends "honor father and mother" against Corban tradition
   - Sounds new: Jesus asserting authority over tradition sounds like he's making new law
   - Torah: Exod 20:12, Deut 5:16 (Fifth Commandment)
   - Connection: Yeshua defends Torah against human tradition. Not creating new law—protecting original law from being nullified.

#### Torah Deepening (Address Objections)

4. **Murder Starts in the Heart**
   - Yeshua: Matt 5:21-22 "You have heard 'do not murder,' but I say whoever is angry..."
   - Sounds new: "But I say" sounds like Jesus overriding Moses with his own authority
   - Torah: Lev 19:17 "You shall not hate your brother in your heart"
   - Connection: Torah already prohibited internal hatred. Yeshua teaching what the murder commandment always included—anger is the seed, murder is the fruit. Torah explication, not innovation.

5. **Adultery Starts with Lust**
   - Yeshua: Matt 5:27-28 "You have heard 'do not commit adultery,' but I say whoever looks with lust..."
   - Sounds new: Jesus adding internal thought-crime to external act
   - Torah: Exod 20:17 "You shall not covet your neighbor's wife"
   - Connection: Tenth Commandment already addressed internal desire. Yeshua showing the connection between coveting (Torah) and adultery (Torah). Explicating, not adding.

6. **Eye for Eye → Turn the Other Cheek**
   - Yeshua: Matt 5:38-39 "You have heard 'eye for eye,' but I say do not resist an evil person"
   - Sounds new: Jesus replacing Torah's harsh justice with radical mercy
   - Torah: Exod 21:24, Lev 24:20 (limiting vengeance principle, not mandating retaliation)
   - Connection: "Eye for eye" is a judicial standard limiting punishment to proportionality, not a personal vengeance mandate. Yeshua teaching personal mercy in relationships—distinct from judicial contexts. Not replacing Torah, applying it rightly.

7. **Love Your Enemies**
   - Yeshua: Matt 5:43-44 "You have heard 'love neighbor, hate enemy,' but I say love your enemies"
   - Sounds new: Jesus transcending Torah's tribal love with universal love
   - Torah: Exod 23:4-5 (help your enemy's animal), Lev 19:18 (love neighbor), Lev 19:33-34 (love the foreigner as yourself)
   - Connection: "Hate your enemy" is not in Torah—it's Pharisaic tradition. Yeshua corrects the addition and points back to Torah's actual standard: love extends to enemies and foreigners.

8. **The Sabbath Was Made for Man**
   - Yeshua: Mark 2:27 "The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the Sabbath"
   - Sounds new: Jesus loosening Sabbath restrictions, asserting lordship over Torah
   - Torah: Exod 16:29, 20:8-11 (Sabbath as gift of rest, not burden)
   - Connection: Yeshua defending Torah against Pharisaic additions (like prohibiting grain-plucking). Not changing Sabbath law—restoring its original intent as gracious rest.

9. **What Defiles Comes from Within**
   - Yeshua: Mark 7:15-19 "Nothing outside can defile... what comes out defiles"
   - Sounds new: Jesus declaring all foods clean, abolishing dietary law
   - Torah: Lev 11 (clean/unclean animals) vs. hand-washing tradition (not in Torah)
   - Connection: Context is hand-washing traditions, not food laws. "All foods clean" means kosher foods don't become defiling through unwashed hands. Yeshua targeting tradition, not Torah. Internal righteousness (speech, thoughts) matters more than external ritual additions.

10. **Do Not Judge**
    - Yeshua: Matt 7:1-2 "Do not judge, or you will be judged. The measure you use will be measured to you"
    - Sounds new: Jesus forbidding judgment entirely, promoting radical tolerance
    - Torah: Lev 19:15 "judge your neighbor fairly," Deut 1:16-17 (righteous judgment required)
    - Connection: Yeshua not forbidding all judgment—Torah requires righteous judgment. Warning against hypocritical judgment (see Matt 7:3-5, the speck and plank). "Measure you use" echoes Torah's honest measures principle (Lev 19:35-36, Deut 25:13-16). Judge rightly, not hypocritically.

## Writing Standards

**From CLAUDE.md:**

- **Hermeneutical:** OT-first reading. Hebrew primacy. Second Temple context where relevant (Hillel, rabbinic teaching styles).
- **Steelman requirement:** Each example includes "why it sounds new" honest acknowledgment before correction.
- **Language:** Use "Yeshua" in theological contexts. Preferred Torah terminology. No Hebrew decoration—only where semantic weight matters.
- **Tone:** Scholarly but accessible. Intellectually confident, not combative. Never condescending.
- **Honesty:** Mark interpretive moves (eye-for-eye judicial/personal distinction, Mark 7 context debate). Don't pretend hard things are easy.
